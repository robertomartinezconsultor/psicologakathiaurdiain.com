Emotional regulation & anger management

Learning to regulate your emotions allows you to respond consciously and calmly to difficult situations. Brief therapy teaches you how to identify triggers and practice self-control techniques.

Signs of emotional dysregulation

  • Explosive or disproportionate reactions.
  • Difficulty calming down after conflict.
  • Feeling overwhelmed easily.
  • Choosing to avoid emotions instead of managing them.
  • Problems communicating your feelings.

How brief therapy helps

You'll learn breathing, mindfulness and assertive communication techniques. We'll also identify patterns that trigger intense reactions and work on replacing them.

Immediate exercise

When you feel angry, breathe deeply for 10 seconds and focus on the sensation of air moving in and out. This reduces the physical response and allows you to respond clearly.
