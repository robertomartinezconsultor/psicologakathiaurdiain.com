Anxiety: how to identify it and what you can do

Anxiety is the most common reason people seek therapy. It shows up as persistent worry, physical symptoms like palpitations, and catastrophic thoughts that prevent you from enjoying the present.

Common symptoms

  • Persistent and uncontrollable worry.
  • Muscle tension, headaches or stomach aches.
  • Avoiding situations out of fear of panic attacks.
  • Trouble falling or staying asleep.
  • Constant sense of impending danger.

How brief therapy helps

In solution-focused brief therapy we use gradual exposure, cognitive restructuring and breathing exercises so you can manage anxiety effectively. Within a few sessions you learn to identify and modify the thoughts that fuel it.
