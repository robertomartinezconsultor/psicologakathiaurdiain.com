Depression: how to identify it and what you can do

Depression is one of the leading reasons people seek therapy. It goes beyond feeling sad: it affects sleep, appetite, concentration and your enjoyment of daily life.

Common symptoms

  • Persistent low mood and sense of emptiness.
  • Loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed.
  • Difficulty concentrating and making decisions.
  • Changes in sleep or appetite.
  • Feelings of guilt or worthlessness.

How brief therapy helps

Brief therapy equips you with tools to identify negative thinking patterns and develop new strategies to cope with depression. We use behavioural activation and cognitive restructuring techniques to help you regain motivation and a sense of purpose.
